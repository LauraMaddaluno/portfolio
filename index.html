Hello, my name is Laura Maddaluno and you are more than welcome on my web page!

I am a web developer self taught student with creative and logical thinking skills. I have spent the last year teaching myself to code because I want to improve people's lives through technology. A bootcamp will enhance my skills and provide me with tools to become a qualified software developer. I am determined to find solutions to the challenges I face, both in life and coding. This makes me feel fulfilled and pushes me to continually improve myself. For example I was able to balance my duties as a full - time mom of a 3 years old with my learning schedule, as a result of which I built my first projects in HTML, CSS, JS using resources like Udemy, freeCodeCamp, codebar and Codecademy. In order to approach backend development, I started learning Ruby. I am a regular student of codebar’s pair programming workshops and I am an active member of that community, volunteering as a coach and being a moderator during the last Codebar Festival event. My singing profession has fine-tuned my transferable skills of communication, self-motivation, working under stressful situations and teamwork. As a team leader between 2008 and 2011, I led a diverse group of singers, musicians and event managers to produce a successful yearly event that attracted larger audiences each year. The performance was enjoyed by each audience and was fulfilling for the musicians. My aim is the same for coding, I adore the idea that people can interact with what I have created and, even better, they can use it. My mindset is based on rules and logical thinking, thanks to my studies in mathematics, Latin and ancient Greek. These skills, combined with my calm, confident and reassuring personality, have meant that I have helped my students with different learning styles to achieve excellent results. My perseverance and attention to detail helped my pupils to improve on their learning styles and the majority of them achieved an A score especially in mathematics. I am resilient and reflective, and rather than waiting for perfection, I use what I have and what I know to produce the best possible results in a given situation. I am aware that not everything can be solved in a particular way, and so I am an attentive listener and a sympathetic person who considers carefully all advice that is given I’m looking for an environment where self-learning is encouraged, but also where structured and professional guidance is available. For these reasons I think Boolean is a tangible opportunity for me to make a final leap and start a career in the tech industry.

What I did before...

Reading books in fancy dress...among musical instruments...?

Since I was a child, I had a passion for singing and my neighborhood can confirm it... I remember taking my broom and doing my show on my grandma's balcony... Then I grew up and started taking singing lessons. After getting my high school diploma, I decided to apply for a masters degree in singing at Conservatorio D. Cimarosa in Avellino. I've done several concerts, some Operas, and also coached the Neapolitan language of Don Pomponio, a buffo character in Rossini's opera La Gazzetta, for The Royal College of Music, in London. I had such a great time... now what? Well now my life has changed and I decided to change career as well.
